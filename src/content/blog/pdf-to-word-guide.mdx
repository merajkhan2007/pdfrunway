PDF files are great for archiving and reading because they lock formatting so layouts stay identical on all screens. However, this same lock makes editing a PDF extremely difficult. 

If you have a document that needs major text edits, layout structural restructuring, or extra tables, the easiest workflow is to convert it back into an editable Microsoft Word document (.docx). 

In this guide, we show you how to convert PDF to Word safely and quickly while keeping your formatting intact.

---

## Why PDF to Word Formatting Breaks

When a document is exported to PDF, its layout is converted into absolute coordinate elements. Instead of reading as "this is a paragraph of text," the PDF reads as "draw character 'H' at coordinates (10, 50), then draw character 'o' at coordinates (18, 50)."

When a converter tries to build a Word document out of this, it has to reconstruct these isolated letters back into logical words, lines, paragraphs, and tables. 

Here are the main reasons why converting formatting can fail:
- **Non-standard Fonts:** If custom typography was used and not embedded properly, spacing can shift.
- **Complex Tables:** Reassembling grid lines and text cells into native Word tables requires advanced structural parsing.
- **Scanned Text (Images):** If your PDF is actually a photocopy or scanned page, the text is stored as pixels, requiring OCR (Optical Character Recognition) to translate the image into characters.

## Reconstructing PDFs Into Word Documents Locally

PDFRunway uses client-side parser algorithms to reconstruct document trees in your browser. All formatting analysis runs inside your browser sandbox.

Here is the conversion workflow:

### Step 1: Upload the PDF
Drag and drop your PDF file into the converter upload area.

### Step 2: OCR Analysis (If Scanned)
If the system detects scanned pages, it runs client-side OCR (via WebAssembly-powered Tesseract.js) to read the text characters from the images.

### Step 3: Layout Parsing
The parser groups text elements into flowing lines, sets up margins, and organizes tables.

### Step 4: Save Docx File
Click **Convert to Word**. Within seconds, your `.docx` file will be generated and saved. You can then open it directly in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, or LibreOffice.

## Best Editors for Word Documents

Once your file is converted, you can edit it with these free tools:
- **Google Docs:** Excellent web-based collaborative editor. Simply upload the docx file and edit.
- **LibreOffice Writer:** A powerful, open-source desktop office suite that has excellent compatibility with docx layouts.
- **Microsoft Word Online:** Free web browser version of MS Word with native formatting rendering.
